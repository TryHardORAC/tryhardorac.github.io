CUHK-Shenzhen Electives I Enjoyed

Jul 2026

A personal shortlist of elective courses I liked at CUHK-Shenzhen, mostly around mathematics, optimization, statistics, and stochastic modeling.

This note only includes elective courses that I personally enjoyed. It does not include required courses. When a course did not belong to my DS study-plan categories, I write "DS Study Plan: None" below.

Mathematics

MAT2050

Mathematical Analysis / 数学分析

3.0 credits · DS Study Plan: Major Elective - Methodology and Theory Stream

Instructor: Ayman Kachmar. The lectures were logically organized and very clear. The instructor put real care into the problem sets, and the handwritten solutions were detailed and pleasant to read.

The workload was high: weekly problem sets and weekly tutorial quizzes. Some problem sets were especially long. Understanding the proofs in class was not easy, but the exams were generally more manageable than the hardest questions from the problem sets.

MAT4220

Partial Differential Equations / 偏微分方程

3.0 credits · DS Study Plan: None

Instructor: Yuan Chen. The course used the instructor's handwritten notes, and the explanations were clear. The workload was on the heavier side, with weekly problem sets, some of which were computationally long.

The course covered first-order PDEs, heat and wave equations, Fourier series, Laplace equations, weak solutions, and Fourier transforms. It was challenging but interesting, and the exams were moderate with a noticeable connection to the problem sets.

Optimization and Numerical Methods

DDA3005

Numerical Methods / 数值方法

3.0 credits · DS Study Plan: Major Elective - Methodology and Theory Stream

Instructor: Andre Milzarek. The lectures were clear, derivation-driven, and often connected to practical algorithmic applications. The final project was a Kaggle-style competition, which made the course feel unusually concrete and fun.

The first half focused on numerical linear algebra, including QR decomposition, SVD, and eigenvalue problems. The second half covered iterative solvers for large-scale linear and nonlinear systems, such as conjugate gradient and Newton-type methods. The workload was moderate, the exams were computation-heavy but fair, and the course was well aligned.

DDA4300

Optimization in Data Science and Machine Learning / 数据科学与机器学习中的优化

1.0 credit · DS Study Plan: None

Instructor: Yinyu Ye. The course introduced optimization ideas through modern research applications, while still building from the fundamentals carefully enough for students to follow. It was a good bridge from coursework to research.

The grade was based on a project. The project topics were interesting and came with detailed guidance: students read papers, reproduced methods, and worked through the ideas step by step. The time commitment depended a lot on how deeply one wanted to push the project.

DDA6110

Advanced Convex Optimization / 高级凸优化

3.0 credits · DS Study Plan: None

Instructor: Yin Zhang. The course began with convex analysis and then moved to classical optimization algorithms not covered in depth in a standard undergraduate optimization class, including interior-point methods, ADMM, proximal-gradient methods, and FISTA.

The workload was high. Theoretical assignments included proof problems from Boyd's book, while coding assignments required reproducing algorithms from lectures or papers, such as a predictor-corrector primal-dual interior-point method for semidefinite programming. The course was demanding, but the handwritten derivations and the instructor's numerical optimization perspective made it very worthwhile.

DDA6040

Dynamic Programming and Stochastic Control / 动态规划与随机控制

3.0 credits · DS Study Plan: None

Instructor: Jianfeng Mao. The course emphasized the high-level picture behind models and algorithms, and the instructor often explained abstract ideas in intuitive language.

The course mainly covered discrete-time dynamic programming, problems with imperfect state information, approximate dynamic programming, and infinite-horizon problems, with several toy examples that helped build intuition. The workload was moderate and the exams were well aligned with the course material.

Statistics and Stochastic Modeling

STA4020

Statistical Modelling in Financial Markets / 金融市场的统计建模

3.0 credits · DS Study Plan: Major Elective - Finance and Economics Stream

Instructor: Chuan Shi. This was a dense and useful course. It touched on econometrics, finance, machine learning, time-series models, statistical inference, stochastic simulation, and some Bayesian ideas. Many topics appeared again in later coursework, so the course felt especially valuable.

The workload was moderate and the exams were aligned with the lectures. Students with a weaker statistics background may need extra time at the beginning because the pace is fast.

STA3020

Statistical Inference / 统计推断

3.0 credits · DS Study Plan: Major Elective - Methodology and Theory Stream

Instructor: Jiasheng Shi. The first half covered sufficient and complete statistics, exponential families, and point estimation; the second half moved to hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and Bayesian inference. The course required a lot of mathematical derivation and probability technique.

This was one of the heaviest undergraduate courses I took. Weekly problem sets were challenging and required sustained thinking, but the instructor was very supportive and offered generous office hours. The course trained my ability to handle long derivations, which helped a lot in later study and research.

DDA6001

Advanced Stochastic Process / 高级随机过程

3.0 credits · DS Study Plan: None

Instructor: Tiefeng Jiang. The course was based on measure-theoretic probability, but it did not require much prior measure theory because the instructor introduced the necessary ideas along the way. The handwritten derivations were smooth, and the lectures were a pleasure to follow.

Topics included the Lindeberg-Feller central limit theorem, conditional expectation, martingales, concentration inequalities, Brownian motion, Ito's formula, and stochastic differential equations. The workload was moderate; some assignments were hard, but the exams were very reasonable.
